Gear Up

Equipment

Here's everything a player needs to get in the water — most of which the club can help you with when starting out.

What You Need

Mask

A low-volume freediving or standard dive mask is essential for seeing underwater. It must fit snugly and create a watertight seal.

Snorkel

A simple J-tube snorkel allows players to breathe at the surface between dives without lifting their head, keeping them ready to dive quickly.

Fins

Long blade freediving fins provide the power needed to push off walls and propel to the pool floor efficiently.

Playing Stick

A flat stick up to 30cm long shaped for pushing the puck. Colour is assigned by team — one dark, one light — for referee identification.

Glove

A protective glove worn on the stick hand guards against contact with the pool floor and opponents' sticks. Mandatory during all play.

Cap & Ear Guards

Numbered water polo caps with built-in ear protectors worn during matches. They identify players and protect the ears during fast underwater play.

The Puck

A lead puck weighing approximately 1.5kg, coated in plastic. Its weight keeps it on the pool floor during play. Provided by the organising club or league.

The Goal

A 3-metre wide metal trough fixed to each end wall at floor level. A goal is scored when the puck is pushed fully inside the trough.

Don't Have Equipment Yet?

When you're starting out the club can lend you a stick and glove. You'll just need your own mask, snorkel, and fins.
