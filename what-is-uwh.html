The Sport

What is Underwater Hockey?

A fast-paced, breath-holding team sport played at the bottom of a swimming pool — unlike anything you've ever tried.

The Basics

Underwater hockey (also known as Octopush) is played at the bottom of a swimming pool between two teams of up to 10 players, with 6 in the water at a time. Players use short sticks (about 30cm) to push a lead puck across the pool floor into the opposing team's goal trough.

Unlike almost any other sport, every action happens completely underwater. Players hold their breath to dive down, compete for the puck, pass to teammates, and surface to breathe — all while the clock ticks.

It rewards swimmers of all abilities. Speed, teamwork, and tactical awareness matter just as much as raw fitness.
