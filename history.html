Origins & Evolution

History of the Sport

From a winter training exercise in 1950s England to a global sport played in over 40 countries.

The Timeline

1954

The Beginning — Southsea, England

Underwater hockey was invented by Alan Blake of the Southsea Sub-Aqua Club — originally called "Octopush" — to keep members active during cold winter months when open-water diving was impractical.

1960s

Spreading Across the UK

The sport quickly caught on with other diving clubs across Britain. Simple equipment and a pool-based format made it easy to organise and accessible to all.

1970s

Going International

The sport spread beyond the UK, taking root in Australia, South Africa, New Zealand, Canada, and parts of Europe before international rules were standardised.

1980

First World Championship

The inaugural Underwater Hockey World Championship was held in Canada, establishing the sport internationally and leading to the formation of CMAS as the world governing body.

1980s–90s

Rules Standardised

International bodies unified equipment dimensions, pool requirements, team sizes, and match duration — enabling genuinely competitive international play for the first time.

2000s

Growth in Ireland

Underwater hockey established itself in Ireland, with clubs forming in Dublin and other cities and the Irish Underwater Hockey League providing a competitive domestic structure.

Today

UCD Krakens

The Krakens compete in the Irish national league and welcome new players of all backgrounds. World Championships are held every two years, drawing teams from over 40 nations.
